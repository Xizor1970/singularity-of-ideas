Interactive Visualisations

See the dynamics in motion — network cascades, power law emergence, and evolutionary competition

1. Idea Cascade Through a Network

What You're Seeing

A network of nodes (minds) connected by edges (relationships). Click "Start Cascade" to introduce an idea at a random node. The idea spreads to connected nodes based on connection strength and the hub influence parameter. Watch how network topology determines which nodes the idea reaches — and which remain untouched.

So What?

This demonstrates why network position matters more than idea quality for spread. The same idea starting at a hub vs. a peripheral node produces dramatically different outcomes. Adjust "Hub Influence" to see how preferential attachment amplifies or dampens the cascade. Real academic networks exhibit exactly this behaviour.

2. Power Law Emergence

What You're Seeing

A citation market. Each dot represents a paper. Each timestep, new papers arrive and cite existing ones. When "Preferential Attachment" is high, popular papers are much more likely to be cited again — creating a rich-get-richer dynamic. The histogram on the right shows the emerging distribution — watch it develop the characteristic long tail of a power law.

So What?

This shows how extreme inequality (1% of papers getting 50% of citations) emerges naturally from a simple rule: "the more cited you are, the more likely you are to be cited again." No conspiracy, no intent — just a mathematical feedback loop. Reduce preferential attachment to zero and the distribution becomes more equal (approaching uniform).

3. Idea Competition & Selection

What You're Seeing

Multiple competing ideas (coloured populations) evolving over time. Each idea's fitness determines its growth rate. Mutations create new variants. When you click "Paradigm Shift!" the fitness landscape inverts — previously fit ideas become unfit and vice versa. Watch how dominant ideas can collapse instantly when the environment changes.

So What?

This visualises Kuhn's paradigm shifts in real time. During "normal science," one idea dominates and alternatives are suppressed. But a sudden environmental change (new technology, crisis, anomaly) can invert the entire landscape. The dominant species becomes endangered; the marginal becomes dominant. This is how deep learning replaced symbolic AI, and how quantum mechanics replaced classical physics.

4. Compression Power

What You're Seeing

Data points (observations) scattered across the canvas. When you click "Generate Theory," a curve (theory) attempts to explain the data. Lower "Theory Simplicity" produces complex curves that fit every point perfectly (overfitting). Higher simplicity produces elegant curves that capture the pattern while missing some noise. The compression ratio — data explained per unit of theory — is displayed.

So What?

This is Occam's Razor visualised. The most powerful theories aren't those that explain every data point (overfitting/epicycles) — they're those that capture the underlying pattern with minimal complexity. Newton's laws are a simple curve that explains billions of data points. Ptolemy's epicycles were a complex curve that explained fewer observations with more parameters.
